Today's Taiwan

 

After 1980s, Taiwan government started to implement different economic policies to change the industrial structure from "heavy industry" to "technology-intensive industry". Technology-intensive industries include optoelectronics, computers, integrated computer manufacturing, and the semiconductor industry. Just like the former processing zones, the government has created a special area for investors to build factories. This special area is called "Science Park". In the Science Park, there are many brands producing a large number of semiconductor products. For example, TSMC, UMC, MediaTek, etc.. The technology headquarters of these Taiwanese brands are located in the Hsinchu Science Park. As reported by the Free Press last year, the total revenue of the Science Park exceeded US$850 trillion in 2018. The semiconductor industry contributes a lot to Taiwan's GDP.
